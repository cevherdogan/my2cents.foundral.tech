Foundral — Prerequisites & Identity

This document captures the foundational prerequisites for building under Foundral in a way that is calm, scalable, and regenerative.

It separates personal identity from organizational continuity, and experiments from permanent structures.

1. The Three-Layer Model

Layer 1 — You (Person)

  • Personal email
  • Personal phone number
  • Private identity

This layer is never mixed with experiments, products, or public-facing work.

Layer 2 — Foundral (Umbrella Identity)

  • One primary Foundral email
  • Primary Foundral domain
  • Future legal entity (Foundral International LLC)

This layer provides continuity across years and projects.

Layer 3 — Projects & Experiments

  • Books
  • Comparative Atlases
  • Donate!!
  • Future initiatives

These are allowed to evolve, pause, or end without disrupting the whole.

2. Email Strategy

Primary Foundral Email (Required)

Create one anchor inbox, for example:

  • hello@foundral.com
  • contact@foundral.com

This email is used for Substack, Gumroad, Amazon KDP, Heyzine, payments, and future legal or banking needs.

Email Aliases (Recommended)

Use aliases instead of separate inboxes:

  • donate@foundral.com
  • atlas@foundral.com
  • books@foundral.com

All aliases forward to the same inbox, preserving clarity without sprawl.

Separate inboxes are only needed if a project becomes a team effort or requires high-volume support.

3. Phone Number

A single phone number is sufficient.

  • Use it for account verification and recovery
  • Do not publish it publicly

A virtual number can be added later if needed.

4. Domain Strategy

Primary Domain

One root domain (e.g. foundral.com) hosts orientation and index pages.

Subdomains for Separation

  • books.foundral.com
  • atlas.foundral.com
  • experiments.foundral.com
  • donate.foundral.com

This mirrors the mental model: book tracks ≠ organizational tracks.

5. Account Prerequisites

Required Before Publishing

  • Foundral email
  • Substack account
  • Gumroad account
  • Amazon KDP account
  • Heyzine account

Optional (Later)

  • Stripe
  • LLC formation
  • Business bank account

6. Decision Rules

  • If it is experimental → alias + subpage
  • If it is permanent → Foundral root
  • If it handles money → Foundral email only
  • If it needs a team → consider separate inbox

7. Closing

This setup favors clarity, containment, and regeneration.

It allows work to mature without pressure, and growth to happen without cleanup.

Foundral is intentionally incomplete.
It will change as the work changes.
